AI deployment has outpaced AI security by years.

Enterprises are racing to integrate large language models into production workflows — from customer-facing chatbots and internal knowledge systems to automated decision engines. Yet the security architecture protecting these deployments remains fundamentally immature. Most organisations have no visibility into prompt injection surfaces, training data poisoning risks, or the cascading failures that a compromised RAG pipeline can trigger across interconnected systems. The gap between deployment velocity and security readiness is where attackers thrive.
